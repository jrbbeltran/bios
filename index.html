Bios

Meet the scholars advancing research, teaching, and leadership in management and organizational behavior.

Monica C. Gavino, Ph.D.

Professor of Human Resources & Diversity

Lucas College of Business, San José State University

Monica C. Gavino is Professor in the Lucas College of Business at San José State University, where she teaches human resources management and diversity in organizations. She brings extensive executive HR experience and consulting expertise into her academic work.

A founding member and Co-President of LaFamilia Management Scholars, her research focuses on DEI in higher education—particularly Latino student success—and human resource management. She has held leadership roles in the Academy of Management DEI Division and multiple equity-focused initiatives.

Jorge A. Gonzalez

Professor of Management & Department Chair

University of Texas Rio Grande Valley

Jorge A. Gonzalez is Professor of Management and Department Chair at the University of Texas Rio Grande Valley. He received his Ph.D. in Management from the Mays Business School at Texas A&M University.

His research, supported by national and international grants, explores diversity and inclusion in organizations, business relationships, work stress, leadership, and cross-cultural organizational behavior. He has published in premier journals including Journal of International Business Studies, Journal of Management Studies, Journal of Organizational Behavior, and Personnel Psychology, and serves as Associate Editor for Journal of Managerial Psychology and Personnel Review.

Mariana J. Lebrón, Ph.D.

Professor of Management & AI Teaching Fellow

Towson University

Mariana J. Lebrón is Professor of Management at Towson University and Inaugural AI Teaching Fellow. Using a power-based framework, she examines how power shapes leadership voice and how AI tools can amplify the voices of those least likely to be heard.

An award-winning educator and innovator (MAACBA 2026 Teaching Innovation Award, University of Maryland Board of Regents Faculty Excellence Award), she develops replicable AI-enhanced and game-based teaching models. She previously served as President of the Eastern Academy of Management and is Co-VP Communications for LaFamilia.

These scholars contribute to advancing inclusive leadership, research excellence, and innovative teaching in management and organizational behavior.
